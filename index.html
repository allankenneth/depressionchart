Depression Graph?

This app is still in very early development...

When Depressgion Graph is fully-developed it will allow you to store your MDI questionnaire results and plot your progress on a timeline. This will allow you to get sense of the direction of your treatment and give you and your doctor consistent information to work with. Your results are stored locally on your device; they are not transmitted over the internet. At a further date, there is a plan to intgrate an opt-in encrypted cloud-based backup service.

The code is open-source on Github, and there are no advertising trackers present on this site anywhere. I have no plans to monetize this service. Your data is yours.

Currently, you can answer the questions and get an accurate summative score. The app is already storing your results on your device, but I am still building the interface to show you these results on a chart. As well, I still investigating a method by which a reminder may be triggered every two weeks, so that the data is well-paced and consistent.

MDI?

The Major Depression Inventory (MDI) is a self-report mood questionnaire developed by the World Health Organization. The instrument was constructed by a team led by Professor Per Bech, a psychiatrist based at Frederiksborg General Hospital in Denmark.[1] The MDI differs from many other self-report inventories, such as the Beck Depression Inventory (BDI), because it is able to generate an ICD-10 or DSM-IV diagnosis of depression in addition to an estimate of symptom severity.[2][3]

Unlike many other similar instruments, the MDI is available free of charge and can be downloaded from the internet with a full manual and scoring instructions. This makes it an attractive option in epidemiological population surveys. It has also been translated into seven languages.
